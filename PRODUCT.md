# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Endurance athletes — runners, cyclists, triathletes — evaluating a training platform. They are data-literate people who own GPS watches and power meters, log every session, and respect tools that measure honestly. Secondary audience: design-aware visitors (the site doubles as a showcase piece), who should leave asking "how was this made?"

## Product Purpose

TrainReady is a training ecosystem: plan sessions, track every discipline (swim / bike / run), sync with Strava, Garmin, TrainingPeaks, and COROS, and peak on race day. This site is its marketing surface and a portfolio-grade experience piece. Copy, features, and imagery may be invented in service of the experience; conversion truthfulness is not a constraint. Success = memorable, distinctive, technically extraordinary.

## Brand Personality

**Calibrated. Engraved. Ready.** The voice of a precision instrument: a Swiss watch spec sheet, a Leica manual, a surveyor's field book. Confidence through exactness, not adrenaline. Numbers are texture. The drama comes from measurement made visible, never from shouting.

## Anti-references

- The previous TrainReady site: dark base + red accent + hero photo + glassmorphism. Nothing carries over except the name and product story.
- The dark-neon fitness template (Whoop / generic Garmin clone / F1-cockpit landing pages). Every sports-tech site is dark; this one is not.
- SaaS landing grammar: centered hero stack, icon-card feature grids, gradient text, hero-metric tiles.
- Editorial-magazine affectation (italic display serif, drop caps). This is an instrument, not a magazine.

## Design Principles

1. **Measurement made visible.** Every decorative element is secretly data: elevation profiles, splits, pace curves, coordinates. Ornament that could not exist without numbers.
2. **Paper, ink, one signal.** A light instrument on warm paper. The accent is flight-recorder orange, the color of devices built to record. It marks what is live or measured, never what is merely emphasized.
3. **Drawn, not faded.** Motion behaves like drafting: lines draw, counters tick, type sets itself. No generic fade-up-on-scroll grammar.
4. **Precision is the spectacle.** The WebGL and GSAP work must feel machined, deliberate, and weightless in performance. If it stutters, it lies about the brand.
5. **One idea per fold.** Long scroll, deliberate pacing, each viewport owns a single thought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on all text (ink on paper clears this easily; orange is never used for body text).
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback: the page composes statically with all content readable, no scroll-driven pinning, no autoplaying canvas motion.
- All meaning carried by color is duplicated in text or shape. Keyboard-navigable throughout.
