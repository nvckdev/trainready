# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Self-coached endurance athletes (triathletes first; runners and cyclists next) evaluating an adaptive training platform. Data-literate age-groupers with GPS watches, power meters, and TrainingPeaks/Strava history, who train early mornings and late evenings around real lives. Secondary audience: design-aware visitors; the site is also the proof that this product is built with more craft than the category's dated incumbents.

## Product Purpose

Taper (see PRD.md) builds and continuously adapts a personalized training plan from a race goal, imported training history, and experience, trained on years of real racing data. The site's job: explain the engine, earn trust through precision and explainability, and convert visitors to the beta waitlist. Claims should track the PRD; illustrative numbers may be invented but must be plausible.

## Brand Personality

**Calibrated. Adaptive. Quietly confident.** The voice of race week: the work is done, the plan is sharp, nothing left to prove. An instrument read at night: lamp off, tomorrow's session already decided. Insider warmth ("trust the taper") under technical exactness. Never hype, never neon, never shouting.

## Anti-references

- The AI-fitness dark reflex: neon green/blue glows, gradient orbs, "AI magic" sparkles, black-and-red gamer cockpits (Whoop, TrainerRoad, generic ML landing pages).
- The category's dated dashboards: dense metric walls, 2015-era SaaS chrome (TriDot, Athletica, Humango screenshots).
- The light-instrument concept (saved on branch `concept/light-instrument`): same measurement DNA, but this is its night edition, not a copy.
- SaaS landing grammar: centered hero stack, icon-card grids, gradient text, testimonial carousels.

## Design Principles

1. **Night edition of the field manual.** Warm ember-black field, bone-cream engraving, hairline rules; the light concept inverted, not replaced. Measurement remains the ornament: contours, splits, load curves.
2. **Orange marks the living.** Flight-recorder signal is reserved for what records, adapts, or counts down: the route, the readiness dial, race day. Never decoration.
3. **Drawn, not faded.** Drafting motion grammar: lines draw, counters tick, type sets itself. Calm exponential ease-outs; nothing bounces.
4. **The plan is the hero.** Every section sells the engine: goal-backed periodization, daily re-flow, the why on every workout. Spectacle exists to demonstrate precision, not to hide its absence.
5. **One idea per fold.** Long scroll, deliberate pacing, race-week quiet.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on the dark field (cream text ≥ 4.5:1; orange only for large text, labels ≥ 4.5:1 via brightened signal tone).
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback: static composed layout, static terrain frame, no pinning.
- Meaning never carried by color alone; keyboard-navigable throughout.
